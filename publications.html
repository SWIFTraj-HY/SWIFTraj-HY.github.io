Capacity drop at active bottlenecks: An empirical study based on trajectory data

Yu Han, Jiarui Wu, Fan Ding, Zhibin Li, Pan Liu, Ludovic Leclercq

Transportation Research Part B: Methodological 2025

Capacity drop, a traffic phenomenon indicating that the discharge flow from a queue is lower than the pre-queue flow, is commonly observed at freeway bottlenecks. In the literature, the majority of empirical studies on capacity drop rely on aggregated traffic flow data. To fully understand the mechanism behind capacity drop, it is essential to analyze trajectory data, which captures the microscopic behavior of individual vehicles. However, the availability of high-quality trajectory data covering both sufficient spatial and temporal scope is limited. Consequently, existing theories and mechanisms to explain capacity drop from the perspective of vehicle behavior are predominantly analytical, lacking direct evidence to validate their impacts on contributing to capacity drop. OpenVTER: An Open Vehicle Trajectory Extraction Framework Based on Rotated Bounding Boxes

Xinkai Ji, Yu Han, Peipei Mao, Yan Huang, Hao Yu, Pan Liu

IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems 2024

Vehicle trajectory data is essential for analyzing and modeling complex traffic behaviors. Although extraction of vehicle trajectory from aerial video data is not a new problem, obtaining trajectories with heading information across various road types, such as intersections or long road segments, requires further research. In this paper, we propose OpenVTER, a generalized Open-source Vehicle Trajectory Extraction framework based on Rotated bounding boxes (RBBs). This framework includes several key components: video stabilization, image division, vehicle detection, vehicle tracking, and data post-processing. Specifically, the rotated vehicle detection model, named YOLOX-R, is applied to detect the small and rotated vehicles using RBBs that provide vehicle heading information.
